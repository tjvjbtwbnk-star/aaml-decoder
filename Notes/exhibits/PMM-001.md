## 1. WELCOME AND INTRODUCTIONS

**MV** welcomed all attendees to the kick-off meeting and expressed BEU's commitment to the successful delivery of the Project. He emphasized the strategic importance of the facility to Borduria's energy infrastructure and the need for strong collaboration between all parties.

**TK** thanked BEU for hosting and confirmed SPCC's readiness to commence work. He introduced the key members of the SPCC team and outlined their respective roles.

**HW** confirmed DPI's role as Engineer under the Contract and outlined the firm's approach to contract administration, emphasizing fairness, technical rigor, and timely decision-making.

All attendees introduced themselves and exchanged business cards. **Action: SPCC to circulate organization chart and contact list (MH).**

---

## 2. CONTRACT ADMINISTRATION PROCEDURES

**AN** presented BEU's expectations regarding contract administration:
- All formal communications to reference Contract clause numbers where applicable
- Use of agreed reference numbering systems
- Document control procedures through designated Document Controllers

**JP** outlined SPCC's proposed procedures, which were generally aligned with BEU's approach. The following was agreed:

**Communication Protocols:**
- Formal notices and letters to be issued by Project Directors or Project Managers
- Technical correspondence may be issued by discipline leads with copy to Project Managers
- Email acceptable for routine communications; formal submissions to be by letter
- Document Controller: Petra Vlková (SPCC), Ana Nikolić (BEU)

**Meeting Schedule:**
- Weekly Progress Meetings: Wednesdays at 10:00 (SPCC site office, once established)
- Monthly Project Management Meetings: Last Monday of each month, alternating venues
- Technical coordination meetings as required

**Submittal Procedures:**
- Design documents to be submitted to Engineer with copy to BEU
- 21-day review period as per Contract unless otherwise agreed
- Format: Both hard copy and electronic (PDF + native CAD formats)

**Payment Procedures:**
- Monthly payment applications by 5th of following month
- Payment certificates within timeframes specified in Contract
- Supporting documentation requirements to be clarified by KH

**Action: AN to issue formal communication procedures document (by 25 January).**

## 3. PROGRAMME REVIEW

**MD** presented a preliminary programme outline. Key dates highlighted:
- Site possession: 1 February 2019
- Basic design approval: Target mid-April 2019
- Civil works commencement: May 2019
- GT1 delivery: March 2020
- Mechanical completion: July 2022
- Taking Over: January 2023

**MV** noted that the programme appears achievable but will require close monitoring, particularly of long-lead equipment procurement.

**HW** requested that the formal baseline programme include greater level of detail, particularly regarding:
- Design deliverables schedule linked to construction activities
- Critical path analysis
- Resource loading
- Weather allowances

**MD** confirmed a detailed baseline programme (Rev 0) would be submitted by end of February 2019.

**Action: SPCC to submit Baseline Programme Rev 0 by 28 February (MD).**

---

## 4. SITE HANDOVER AND MOBILISATION

**IP** confirmed BEU is on track to provide Site possession on 1 February 2019. Site is currently secured and vacant. Access will be via the eastern gate off Route 7.

**PN** outlined SPCC's mobilisation requirements:
- Site establishment: Temporary offices, laydown areas, welfare facilities
- Access roads and hardstanding
- Temporary power and water supplies
- Security arrangements

**BS** confirmed BEU will provide temporary utilities connection points as shown on the Site plan. SPCC responsible for distribution from these points.

**Ground Investigation Programme:**

**DK** emphasized the importance of comprehensive ground investigation given limited existing data. He requested SPCC submit a detailed investigation programme promptly.

**Andrej Kováč (AK)** (SPCC Lead Civil Engineer - joined meeting for this item) confirmed SPCC will submit ground investigation proposal by mid-February, covering:
- Borehole locations and depths
- Laboratory testing scope
- Geotechnical parameters required for foundation design

**HW** noted that ground investigation results may impact foundation design and potentially the programme. He requested the investigation be expedited.

**Action: SPCC to submit Ground Investigation Programme by 15 February (AK).**

**Action: BEU to confirm temporary utilities connection points by 25 January (BS).**

---

## 5. DESIGN AND ENGINEERING

**WB** presented the design submission schedule for Basic Design documents:
- Process design basis: End January
- Civil and structural design: Early February
- Mechanical design: Mid-February
- Electrical design: Late February

All design packages to be submitted in batches for review. SPCC targeting Engineer's approval by mid-April to maintain programme.

**DK** raised concern about the tight schedule for design review. **HW** confirmed DPI will mobilize sufficient resources to meet the 21-day review period but emphasized submissions must be complete and of adequate quality to avoid delays.

**WB** confirmed SPCC's design team is fully resourced and committed to meeting the schedule.

**SJ** requested early engagement on electrical interface matters, particularly HV switchyard design and grid connection requirements. **Sophie Dumont (SD)** (SPCC Lead Electrical Engineer) agreed to coordinate directly with SJ.

**Action: SPCC to submit Design Deliverables Schedule by 25 January (WB).**

**Action: SD and SJ to arrange technical coordination meeting on electrical interfaces by end January.**

---

## 6. HSE AND QUALITY

**PH** presented an overview of SPCC's approach to HSE management:
- Project HSE Plan to be submitted by end January
- Target: Zero lost-time incidents
- Weekly HSE meetings
- Comprehensive induction programme for all site personnel

**DI** expressed satisfaction with the proposed approach and confirmed BEU's HSE requirements. He emphasized that Bordurian regulations must be fully complied with.

**Ondřej Malý (OM)** (SPCC Quality Manager) outlined the Quality Management approach:
- ISO 9001 certified processes
- Inspection and Test Plans for all major activities
- Independent third-party inspection where specified
- Document control and record-keeping

**DK** requested that quality procedures be fully aligned with contract specifications.

**Action: SPCC to submit Project HSE Plan by 30 January (PH).**

**Action: SPCC to submit Project Quality Plan by 15 February (OM).**
