## 1. Executive Summary

This Quality Management Plan (QMP) establishes the quality management system for the Borduria Combined Cycle Power Plant Project. It defines the quality objectives, organisation, procedures, and controls to be implemented throughout design, procurement, construction, and commissioning phases.

The QMP has been developed in accordance with ISO 9001:2015 and the Contract requirements (Employer's Requirements, Volume 3, Section 7).

This document is submitted for Employer approval as required under Sub-Clause 4.9 of the Contract.

---

## 2. Introduction

### 2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Quality Management Plan is to:

1. Define the quality management system for the Project
2. Establish quality objectives and targets
3. Describe the organisation and responsibilities for quality
4. Specify the quality procedures and controls
5. Define inspection, testing, and documentation requirements

### 2.2 Scope

This QMP applies to all Works under the Contract, including:

- Engineering and design
- Procurement of equipment and materials
- Civil and structural works
- Mechanical installation
- Electrical and instrumentation installation
- Pre-commissioning and commissioning
- Performance testing

### 2.3 References

- Contract No. BEU-BCCPP-2018-001
- Employer's Requirements, Volume 3 - Quality Requirements
- ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management Systems
- ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management Systems
- ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety

## 3. Quality Policy

### 3.1 SPCC Quality Policy Statement

"SPCC is committed to delivering the Borduria Combined Cycle Power Plant to the highest standards of quality, meeting all contractual requirements and stakeholder expectations. We will achieve this through:

- Rigorous application of our quality management system
- Continuous improvement in all processes
- Engagement and competence of our personnel

### 5.2 Key Procedures Summary

**QP-007 Non-Conformance Management:**

Non-conformances shall be documented on NCR forms and classified as:
- Category 1: Critical - affecting safety or performance
- Category 2: Major - affecting quality but correctable
- Category 3: Minor - cosmetic or documentation

Disposition options: Rework, Repair, Use-As-Is (with concession), Reject

**QP-011 Welding Quality Control:**

- All welders qualified to EN ISO 9606
- Welding procedures qualified to EN ISO 15614
- NDT as per EN ISO 17635
- Acceptance criteria per EN ISO 5817 (Level B for pressure parts)

## 6. Inspection and Test Plans

### 6.1 ITP Structure

Inspection and Test Plans (ITPs) shall be developed for each work package. ITPs shall identify:

- Inspection activities and acceptance criteria
- Hold points (H) - work cannot proceed without Engineer witness
- Witness points (W) - Engineer notified; may attend
- Review points (R) - documentation review only
- Responsible parties

### 6.2 Key ITPs
