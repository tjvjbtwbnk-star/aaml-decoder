## RE: BORDURIA COMBINED CYCLE POWER PLANT PROJECT – SUBMISSION OF BASELINE PROGRAMME REV 0

In accordance with Sub-Clause 8.3 of the Contract and as committed at Project Management Meeting No. 1, I am pleased to submit the Baseline Programme Rev 0 for the Works.

**Enclosed Documents:**
1. Baseline Programme Rev 0 (Drawing No. SPCC-PROG-002, Rev 0) - A1 size, scale 1:50
2. Programme Narrative Document (42 pages)
3. Critical Path Analysis Report
4. Resource Histogram
5. Programme Basis and Assumptions Document

**Programme Overview:**

The Baseline Programme sets out the detailed sequence and timing of all activities required to complete the Works within the Time for Completion of 1,460 days from the Commencement Date (15 January 2019), achieving Taking Over on 14 January 2023.

**Key Features:**

- **Activity Detail:** Programme broken down to 2-3 week activity level (1,247 activities)
- **Logic Links:** Full precedence network with logical relationships
- **Critical Path:** Identified and highlighted (current duration: 1,460 days)
- **Float Analysis:** Free float and total float calculated for all activities
- **Resources:** Labor histograms showing resource deployment by discipline
- **Milestones:** 48 key milestones identified and tracked

**Critical Path Summary:**

The current critical path runs through:
1. Basic Design approval (Jan-Apr 2019) - 105 days
2. Foundation design and approval (Apr 2019) - 15 days
3. Piling and foundation works (May-Sep 2019) - 153 days
4. Gas turbine procurement and delivery (Current-May 2020) - 471 days
5. GT1 installation (Jun-Sep 2020) - 122 days
6. HRSG1 installation (Jun-Dec 2020) - 183 days
7. Steam turbine installation (Jan-Mar 2021) - 61 days
8. Commissioning preparations (Apr-Aug 2022) - 153 days
9. First fire GT1 (Sep 2022) - 30 days
10. Performance testing (Nov 2022-Jan 2023) - 76 days

**Programme Changes from Preliminary Programme:**

This Baseline Programme supersedes the Preliminary Programme (SPCC-PROG-001, Rev P01) submitted 22 January 2019. Key changes:

1. Greater level of detail (1,247 activities vs. 284 in preliminary)
2. Updated based on supplier delivery confirmations
3. Reflects approved ground investigation schedule
4. Incorporates design review cycle timing
5. Includes detailed civil works breakdown
6. Updated resource loading based on subcontractor input

We request the Engineer's review and approval of this Baseline Programme in accordance with Sub-Clause 8.3.
