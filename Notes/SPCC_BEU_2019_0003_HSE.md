## RE: BORDURIA COMBINED CYCLE POWER PLANT PROJECT – SUBMISSION OF PROJECT HSE PLAN

In accordance with Sub-Clause 4.8 of the Contract and as committed at Project Management Meeting No. 1, I am pleased to submit the Project Health, Safety and Environment Plan for your review and approval.

**Document Submitted:**
- Project HSE Plan (Document No. SPCC-HSE-001, Rev 0) - 124 pages

**HSE Policy and Objectives:**

SPCC is committed to the highest standards of health, safety and environmental performance on the BCCPP Project. Our HSE policy establishes the following objectives:

**Safety Objectives:**
- Zero fatalities
- Zero lost-time incidents
- Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) target: <0.5 per million man-hours
- Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) target: <2.0 per million man-hours

**Environmental Objectives:**
- Full compliance with Bordurian environmental regulations
- Minimize construction impact on local community
- Effective waste management and recycling
- Protection of River Vlaja water quality

**Plan Structure:**

The HSE Plan is organized as follows:

**Section 1: HSE Management System Overview**
- Policy statements
- Organization and responsibilities
- Legal and regulatory compliance framework

**Section 2: Risk Assessment and Management**
- Hazard identification methodology
- Risk assessment matrices
- High-risk activities and controls

**Section 3: Health and Occupational Hygiene**
- Occupational health programme
- Medical surveillance
- Hygiene monitoring (noise, dust, chemical exposure)

**Section 4: Safety Management**
- Permit-to-Work system
- Hot work procedures
- Work at height procedures
- Confined space entry procedures
- Lifting operations and crane safety
- Electrical safety
- Excavation and trenching safety
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements

**Section 5: Emergency Preparedness and Response**
- Emergency response plan
- Evacuation procedures
- Fire prevention and protection
- First aid and medical emergency response
- Emergency contact lists

**Section 6: Environmental Management**
- Environmental aspects and impacts register
- Dust control measures
- Noise management
- Waste management plan
- Spill prevention and response
- Erosion and sediment control
- Water quality protection (River Vlaja)

**Section 7: Training and Competency**
- Site induction requirements
- Toolbox talks programme
- Specialized training for high-risk activities
- Competency verification system

**Section 8: Incident Management**
- Incident reporting and investigation procedures
- Root cause analysis methodology
- Corrective and preventive actions
- Lessons learned dissemination

**Section 9: Monitoring and Performance Measurement**
- HSE inspections and audits
- KPI monitoring and reporting
- Management review process

**Section 10: Interfaces and Coordination**
- Coordination with subcontractors
- Interface with BEU and Engineer
- Community liaison

**Key Features of Our HSE Approach:**

1. **Dedicated HSE Resources:** Full-time HSE Manager (Peter Horváth) and site-based HSE Officers (initially 2, scaling to 5 during peak construction)

2. **Site Induction:** Comprehensive induction programme (minimum 4 hours) for all personnel before site access. Includes:
   - Project-specific hazards and controls
   - Emergency procedures
   - Environmental requirements
   - Bordurian regulations and cultural considerations

3. **Permit-to-Work System:** Strict permit requirements for:
   - Hot work (welding, cutting, grinding)
   - Work at height (>2m)
   - Confined space entry
   - Excavation (>1.2m depth)
   - Lifting operations (>5 tons)
   - Electrical work

4. **Daily Safety Meetings:** Each work crew to conduct daily toolbox talk (minimum 15 minutes) before work commencement

5. **Weekly HSE Meetings:** Site-wide HSE coordination meeting every Monday at 08:00

6. **HSE Inspections:**
   - Daily walk-through inspections by HSE Officers
   - Weekly formal inspections with checklist
   - Monthly comprehensive audits
   - Random spot checks

7. **Incident Reporting:** All incidents (including near-misses) to be reported within 24 hours. Investigation to commence immediately for serious incidents.

**Compliance with Bordurian Regulations:**

The HSE Plan has been developed to ensure full compliance with:
- Bordurian Occupational Health and Safety Act (2016)
- Bordurian Environmental Protection Act (2015)
- Bordurian Construction Safety Regulations (2017)
- Municipal Ordinances for Industrial Zone Klow-East
- All relevant Bordurian National Standards

A detailed compliance matrix is included in Appendix A of the HSE Plan.

**Subcontractor Management:**

All subcontractors will be required to:
- Comply with this HSE Plan
- Submit their own HSE plans for approval before mobilization
- Participate in coordination meetings
- Report incidents through SPCC's system
- Attend SPCC safety inductions and toolbox talks

**Community and Stakeholder Engagement:**

We recognize the project's proximity to residential areas and will implement:
- Advance notification of noisy activities
- Dust suppression measures on access roads
- Traffic management plan for delivery vehicles
- Community complaints register and response system
- Regular stakeholder updates on HSE performance

**Review and Approval:**

We request BEU's review and approval of this HSE Plan within 14 days to allow us to commence implementation activities including:
- Procurement of safety equipment and PPE
- Finalization of site induction materials
- Training of HSE personnel
- Setup of HSE management systems and databases

Should you require any clarification or wish to discuss any aspect of the HSE Plan, please contact me directly or arrange a meeting with our HSE team.

SPCC is fully committed to delivering this project without harm to people or the environment, and we look forward to working collaboratively with BEU to achieve exemplary HSE performance.
